Borda count, points assigned in form of n-p, where n = number of options and p = position on ballot.
The highest-ranked option gets n - 1 points.
The lowest-ranked option gets 0 points (n - n).
Particularly susceptible to distortion through the presence of options which are mostly irrelevant (rarely come into consideration) and strategic voting.
Dowdall / Nauru system. Very similar to Borda count, but is more favorable to options with many first preferences.
Points assigned in form of 1/p, where p = position on ballot - the first-ranked option gets 1 point, the second gets 1/2 of a point, the third 1/3 of a point, etc.
